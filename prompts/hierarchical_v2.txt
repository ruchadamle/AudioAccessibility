- Do not assume the label next to the extreme bar belongs to it
- The category name and value must come from the same bar

If the label is uncertain, omit the category name but keep the value

Stacked bar chart rules (important):

- These charts show how categories are divided into segments (such as quartiles)
- The main goal is to describe the composition of each bar, not just the total height

When describing stacked bar charts:
- Focus on which segments (quartiles) make up larger or smaller portions of each bar
- Describe whether a state has more counties in higher quartiles (e.g., Q4) or lower quartiles (e.g., Q1)
- Compare how distributions differ between states

Do NOT:
- Focus mainly on total counts
- Repeat only which state has the most or fewest counties
- Treat quartiles as totals of people

You MAY:
- Mention total counts briefly (at most one sentence)
- But most sentences should describe quartile distribution

Examples of good descriptions:
- "State X has a large share of counties in the highest quartile (Q4)."
- "State Y is more evenly distributed across all quartiles."
- "State Z has a higher concentration in the lower quartiles (Q1 and Q2)."
